Ninjas in Pajamas (NiP: Ninja in pajamas) is a Swedish cybersport organization, founded in 1999. The project was closed in 2007, but again in 2012 it continued its work. Currently represented by units for Dota 2, Counter-Strike: Global Offensive, League of Legends, Overwatch and Paladins. In the early 2000s NiP was one of the most successful in the discipline of Counter-Strike, according to GotFrag. The founders were: Sweden Tommy "Potti" Ingemarsson (Tommy Ingemarsson) and Sweden Emil "HeatoN" Christensen (Emil Christensen). Their first success was the 1 st place at the CPL World Championship 2001.

"The project was conceived as a sort of lan-team with bullet and elajten - members who were not with us in the Lana (I knew them" long ago "from the Internet). We all played in a clan called [OF], which was purely a clan lan, but we added bullet and elajten to our squad. The clan leader in that team was weak in the management of the team and not particularly interested in the game, so we decided to start something ourselves and I came up with NiP. All this was new for me and I had to turn to tootzi for help in creating a clan site (which was needed for registration on clanbase) and other things. After that, we began to expand and take into the team people who were not with us in Lana."
